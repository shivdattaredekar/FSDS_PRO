Shivaji Maharaj, born in 1630, was a formidable Maratha warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. Renowned for his progressive administration and military acumen, he established a competent navy and an efficient governance system that integrated various communities. His strategic prowess, including guerrilla tactics, enabled him to defend his kingdom against the Mughal Empire and other adversaries. Shivaji's leadership and visionary policies promoted the welfare of his subjects, and he is celebrated as a champion of Hindu resilience and a pioneer of modern administration in India. His legacy continues to inspire leadership and valor
